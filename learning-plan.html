My Learning Plan

Bootcamp (as at: 06/03/2022)

Career Goals

My long term goal is to have a career that is meaningful, sustainable, involves regular collaboration with a team that has trust in each other and that supports each other's growth and development. I want to have a career that involves art and design, and is serving a greater good. I want to be building things that help people. I'd like to achieve a healthy work/life balance, and part of this is doing work that I feel has a positive purpose, and working with companies that care about their employees as human beings. I want to work for a company that invests in it's employee's development, and that has an awesome workplace culture that I can be a part of and positively contribute to. A goal of mine is to be able to travel to different countries (particularly to be able to go to the US more often), and to be able to take my work with me if I feel it would be productive to do so. Part of this is a long term goal, to be able to work independently at times with some flexibility in creating my own schedule. I'd like a career that pays well, so that I can take care of my and my family's human needs without stress, and so that I can also do nice things for myself and for others, and to be able to contribute to good causes.

My Future Plans

I hope to use the skills I gain to build beautifully designed, easy to navigate, useful websites and applications for companies that are building them to benefit the end-user first.

I want to use my time to create things for the good of the people. Websites and apps that will make it easier for people to do things that they want to, understand things that will be beneficial to have knowledge of, and bring more joy and beauty into their lives.

When I leave Dev Academy, I want to be a person who can bring skills, knowledge, and ideas to the table. I want to be a person who meets others with enthusiasm, who helps lift the energy and is caring and empathetic in all of my interactions.

I have my eye on several companies! While my research is still underway, I've found Wellington companies who have a focus on clear, user-centered design and who illustrate a genuine care about the people who work at their company. It's so exciting to read about what kind of people companies are looking for, and to think about whether I could be one of them at the end of this course.

Strengths

  • My biggest strength is empathy. I care about other people and I believe that I am good at sensing other people.
  • Collaboration is a skill that I have practiced in and I believe will be useful.
  • My attention to detail and design background will likely be strengths that I will draw upon.

Weaknesses

  • My attention to detail can also be a weakness. I need to be careful that I employ this at the right time, rather than too early in the process.
  • My biggest limitation is my technical skills. I thought that I had good general technical skills with computers, but web development requires a whole new set of computer skills. Foundations has taught me that I need more practice with coding to have the technical skills required to be successful in this career path.
  • Getting into the web developer mindset so that I can be effective at problem solving in my coding, is a muscle that I need to exercise more. I need to be able to move past any initial anxiousness that I may encounter and view problem solving with an outlook of curiosity and wonder.
  • Fear is another weakness that can sometimes arise - fear of failure.

To manage my workload in this program, I will manage myself by:

  • Staying focused when I am working
  • Putting in my best effort
  • Getting enough sleep

Skills to Develop

As an introverted cancerian, it can take me a while to feel comfortable around new people, but I don't like hanging out in my shell all the time. I find that my interactions are better with people when I am playful and open, rather than quiet and shy. I hope that this career pathway increases my self-confidence and allows me to truly be myself at all times.

I would also like to develop my leadership skills. I have been around a lot of strong leaders and feel that I have some of the skills required to become a good leader, but again, I need to increase my confidence in this area.

I would love to get a better handle on my time management skills. I have developed some perfectionistic tendencies through my prior study and work experience. This has served me well in circumstances where attention to detail is critical, but I also feel that perfectionism can get in the way of moving forward. Done is better than perfect. Sometimes wanting perfection prevents me from getting things done in a timely fashion, which increases my stress levels.

It would be amazing to know how to work with difficult personalities productively and in a way that doesn't affect me emotionally.
